    Cardiology

    Cardiology is a branch of internal medicine. A cardiologist is not the same as a cardiac surgeon.

    Cardiology studies such pathological conditions as ischemic heart disease (IHD), hypertension, congenital heart defects, acquired heart defects, cerebrovascular diseases and others. Today, the share of cardiological pathology in the structure of mortality in the population of developed countries is 40-60%, while the incidence continues to rise.

    Neurology

    Deals with the occurrence of diseases of the central and peripheral parts of the nervous system, and also studies the mechanisms of their development.

    Neurology, as the science of the structure and function of the nervous system, is based on evolutionary theory and the research of physiologists of the late 19th and early 20th centuries. As a clinical discipline, neurology studies pathological changes in the central and peripheral, including the autonomic nervous system and muscle diseases, as well as neuromuscular coupling.

    Hepatology

    Hepatology is the science of liver diseases that can be infectious and non-infectious in nature.

    Hepatology has developed as a separate section of gastroenterology as a result of the specialization of doctors and medical institutions in diseases of the liver and biliary tract. Methods of related disciplines based on the achievements of modern physics and mathematics were involved in the study of diseases.

    Pediatrics

    There is a science about the distinctive features, functions and diseases of the child's body and based on those features, the preservation of health and the treatment of diseases in children.

    The tasks of preventive medicine are to recreate health in healthy people according to the formula: healthy parents - healthy lifestyle and work - healthy child. Researchers have proven that a child is a constantly evolving organism from birth to adulthood.
